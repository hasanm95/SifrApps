## 1. Freedom: The Legacy Giant

Freedom is the "old guard" of the industry. It's great because it works across your Mac, Windows, and phone all at once.

- **The Good:** Excellent for writers who need to lock down their entire digital ecosystem.
- **The Catch:** The mobile app can feel a bit "clunky" and outdated compared to newer tools. Users often report accidentally locking themselves out because the interface is confusing.
- **Price:** Around $40/year.

## 2. AppBlock: The Direct Competitor

If you just want a functional tool to shut things down on Android, AppBlock is a very popular choice.

- **The Good:** It has robust features like "Strict Mode" and even location-based blocking.
- **The Catch:** The free version is very restricted, constantly pushing you toward a subscription. Also, if you use a phone with an aggressive battery saver (like Xiaomi or Samsung), the system often "kills" AppBlock, letting you bypass your own rules.

## 3. One Sec: The Psychological Tool

One Sec takes a different approach. Instead of a hard block, it forces you to take a deep breath before an app opens.

- **The Good:** It's scientifically proven to help break the dopamine loop by adding friction.
- **The Catch:** For people with severe ADHD or deep-rooted habits, a "breathing exercise" is often too easy to skip when the craving for a scroll hits.
